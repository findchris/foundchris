<h3>Funny Analogies First</h3>
<ul>
	<li>Discovery Channel is like Whole Foods:  A brand you can depend on to provide quality nourishment.</li>
	<li>"America's Funniest Home Videos" is like a snack machine:  Providing little nutritional value.</li>
	<li>Consuming TMZ is like consuming cigarettes:  Bad for you and you know it!</li>
	<li>Rusch Limbaugh is like alcohol, causing biligerence and a propensity for emotion-fueled conflict.</li>
</ul>
<h3>Too Much Junk</h3>
There is too much junk food and too much junk media available for consumption.

Walk down the snack aisle of your grocer to witness how much nutrition-free pseudo food is on offer.  Depending on your grocer, the ratio of of real, whole food to boxed, processed food is available probably around 10-to-1 (based on the total number of aisles, minus the fresh produce, meat, and dairy).  Despite all of the fake foods claiming they are nutritious using flashy graphics on their packaging (e.g. "Low-fat", "Whole Grain", "Heart Healthy", "Fat free", "100% Daily Value of Vitamin C"), they will never be as healthy as what you'll find in the organic produce section.
